WordPress powers a huge share of the web for a reason. It's flexible and your team can update content without calling a developer every time. The problem is most WordPress sites get built with a pile of plugins and page-builder bloat that slows everything down.

We build WordPress sites with a lighter footprint: custom themes where it makes sense, a sensible editing experience for your team, and only the plugins you actually need.

## What's Included

- Custom theme development, or careful customization of an existing theme
- Custom plugin development for functionality that off-the-shelf plugins can't handle well
- Editorial setup so your team can actually update the site without breaking it
- Performance and security hardening
- Migration from other platforms, or from a messy existing WordPress install
- Ongoing maintenance, updates, and support

## Who This Is For

Businesses that want the flexibility of WordPress without the usual plugin-bloat slowdown and want a site their own team can manage day-to-day.

## How We Work

1. **Audit or Plan** — For existing sites: a plugin/performance/security review to find what's slowing things down. For new builds: content structure and editor-experience planning before any code.
2. **Scope & Quote** — Fixed written scope. If we think you don't need custom development — that a well-configured existing theme would do — we'll say so.
3. **Staging Build** — Everything happens on a staging copy. Your live site stays untouched until you approve.
4. **Editor Setup** — We configure the block editor (or your preferred workflow) so your team can update pages without breaking layouts — the part most WordPress builds skip.
5. **Launch, Harden & Handover** — Deploy, security hardening (login protection, updates strategy, backups), and a walkthrough of how to manage your own content.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**My WordPress site is slow. Can you fix it without rebuilding?**
Usually. The most common causes — bloated page builders, unoptimized images, too many plugins, cheap hosting — are all fixable incrementally. We audit first and only recommend a rebuild when patching genuinely costs more than starting over.

**How many plugins is too many?**
It's less about the count and more about the quality and overlap. We routinely replace 3-4 single-purpose plugins with a few lines of custom code, and we avoid plugins that inject their own page builders or tracking.

**Will my team be able to edit the site?**
That's the point of choosing WordPress. We set up editing so the common tasks — new page, new post, swap images, update text — are safe and obvious, and the fragile parts are locked down.

**Do you handle WooCommerce?**
Yes, for stores that are committed to WordPress. If you're starting an online store from scratch, we'll often recommend [Shopify](/services/shopify-development) instead — we'll walk you through the tradeoffs honestly.
